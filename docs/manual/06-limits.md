# Chapter 06: Limits

**Contents:** What defensibility and "verified" do not guarantee; critical areas.

---

## Defensibility is not truth

Defensibility is **structural and policy-relative**: how well a claim holds up given the *recorded* evidence and links and your policy rules. We do **not** assert that a claim is true or false. See [Critical area 01](../../critical_areas/01-defensibility-is-not-truth.md).

---

## Source independence is not verified

"Independent sources count" is the number of *distinct sources in the model*, not verified real-world independence. See [Critical area 02](../../critical_areas/02-source-independence-is-not-verified.md).

---

## Verified = structure and hashes

"Verified" means the .chronicle package is structurally valid and evidence files match their hashes. It does **not** mean claims are true or that evidence actually supports them. See [Critical area 03](../../critical_areas/03-what-the-verifier-checks.md) and [Verification guarantees](../verification-guarantees.md).

---

## Read the critical areas

Before relying on scores or verification in production or research, read the [Critical areas](../../critical_areas/README.md). They spell out all high-risk boundaries (evidence–claim linking, policy/thresholds, actor identity) so the system is not over-trusted.

**Summary:** Chronicle gives you a defensibility score and a verifiable package; it does not certify truth, verify source independence, or validate that evidence actually supports the claim. Use it to *show* and *score* support; use your own review and policy for decisions.
