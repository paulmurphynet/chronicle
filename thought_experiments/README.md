# Thought experiments

This folder contains **thought experiments**: hypothetical scenarios we use to stress-test Chronicle’s design, limits, and documentation from different angles. They are not user stories or feature specs—they are reflective exercises that help us see what we got right and what we might improve.

**Audience:** Contributors, reviewers, and anyone who wants to reason about Chronicle from a conceptual or cross-disciplinary perspective (e.g. epistemology, evaluation, compliance).

---

## Index

| # | Title | What it explores |
|---|--------|------------------|
| [01](01-epistemologists-conference-review.md) | Epistemologists’ conference review | Four epistemologist PhDs (social, formal, virtue, applied) evaluate the project: what is correctly implemented from an epistemic standpoint, and what could be improved, and why. |

---

## How to use these

- **Before design decisions:** Skim the relevant thought experiment to see which assumptions or limits are being probed.
- **When documenting limits:** Use the scenarios to check that critical areas and epistemology-scope stay aligned with how outside experts might read the system.
- **For discussion:** Treat them as conversation starters in reviews or RFCs, not as binding requirements.

---

**Relationship to other docs:** [Critical areas](../critical_areas/README.md) state what the system does and does not guarantee; [Epistemology scope](../docs/epistemology-scope.md) lists what we cover and what we don’t. Thought experiments explore those boundaries through hypothetical reviewers and situations.
